The Brain's Organization

The Brain is a highly organized structure containing three types of centers. Each center is responsible for the different actions we carry out in our everyday lives.

1.Sensory Center:
This is responsible for receiving incoming messages from the sensory organs of the human body.
2.Motor Centers:
This is responsible to carry the instructions from the brain to the effectors, such as muscles and glands of the human body.
3.Association Centers:
This is responsible to interpret the information delivered to the sensory centers and make sure that the proper instructions are given via the motor centers.
